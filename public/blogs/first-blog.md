# Building Autonomous Unmanned Ground Vehicles

Autonomous ground vehicles (UGVs) are at the core of modern robotics research, combining perception, decision-making, and control into a single intelligent system.

At **UGV Tech Team, DTU**, our goal is to design vehicles that can operate reliably in real-world environments with minimal human intervention.

---

## Why Autonomous UGVs Matter

UGVs have applications across many domains:

- Space exploration and planetary rovers  
- Defense and reconnaissance  
- Disaster response and search & rescue  
- Autonomous logistics and delivery  

By focusing on autonomy, we aim to push the limits of what ground robots can achieve.

---

## Core Building Blocks

An autonomous UGV typically consists of:

### 1. Perception  
Sensors such as cameras, LiDAR, IMU, and GPS help the robot understand its surroundings.

### 2. Localization  
Algorithms like SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) allow the robot to estimate its position accurately.

### 3. Planning  
Path planning and obstacle avoidance algorithms decide *where* the robot should move.

### 4. Control  
Low-level controllers translate planned paths into motor commands.

## Our Approach at UGV DTU
